Touch targets
Minimum 36px height for all interactive elements on desktop. 44px for primary actions. This is non-negotiable — do not shrink buttons, inputs, or table row heights below these values.

05 — Foundation
Elevation & Depth
Four levels of surface elevation. Achieved through border weight and subtle shadow — never through background darkening. The hierarchy communicates rank, not decoration.

06 — Components
Buttons & Actions
Every button communicates the weight and consequence of its action. Primary actions are navy — they carry authority. Destructive actions are crimson-tinted, never navy. Gold is reserved for promotion and rank actions only.
Variants
Sizes
Button hierarchy rule: Only one Primary button per screen section. If two actions appear side-by-side, the lesser action is Ghost. Destructive buttons must never be Primary — they are always Danger variant with a confirmation step before any API call fires.

Stat card rule: The value on a stat card never shows decimal precision unless it is a percentage (then max 1 decimal). Always use toLocaleString() for numbers above 999. The subtext must provide context — a number without context is noise.
11 — Components
Feedback & Toasts
System feedback messages are minimal and direct. A coloured dot encodes the category — no icons, no heavy colours, no emoji. Every message uses active voice and tells the cadet or officer exactly what happened.
Examination published. Cadets can now attempt from 12 Apr 09:00 onwards.
Attempt blocked — Cadet AP/TRP/01236 has already submitted this examination.
Auto-saving answers. Last saved at 10:34:22.
12 minutes remaining. Answers are being saved automatically.
Toast messages appear bottom-right, stack upward, and auto-dismiss after 5 seconds. Errors do not auto-dismiss — the cadet must acknowledge them. Toasts that confirm background saves (auto-save) use Info, not Success — success is reserved for cadet-initiated actions.
12 — Identity
Voice & Tone
The portal speaks with the measured authority of the NCC. Precise, respectful, never casual. It never over-explains; it assumes the cadet knows their duty. Error messages are direct without being harsh.
Use — clear & precise
Examination submitted. Your score will be available shortly.
You do not have permission to access this examination.
Session expired. Sign in again to continue.
12 questions answered. 8 questions remaining.
Cadet AP/TRP/01234 has been suspended.
Avoid — vague or casual
Oops! Something went wrong. Please try again!
You can't do this right now.
Uh oh — your session timed out.
Almost done! Just a few more questions to go!
User has been disabled successfully!

14 — Identity
Iconography
Use Lucide React exclusively. Icons at 16px for inline/label use, 20px for navigation, 24px max for empty state illustrations. Never use filled icons — outline-only maintains the minimal, precise aesthetic. Never use icons as the sole indicator of meaning — always pair with a text label.
